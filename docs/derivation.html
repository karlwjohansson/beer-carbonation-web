The intent of this calculator

Some beers benefit from higher levels of CO2. If you want to partially carbonate a beer in a keg and the bottle you might want to add sugar to the beer in order to reach a higher level of CO2 in the bottle this calculator can provide you an estimated amount of sugar to add. The equations are assumed to operate in ideal conditions which is obviously not how reality works. Depening on your method of transfer/adding sugar etc. there are losses which are not accounted for in the equations. Therefore the amount of sugar might not land you the precise amount of CO2 stated. Use the amount of sugar as an entery point and then experiment to reach you prefered carbonation. The calculation of sugar is divided into three steps:
  • Calculate the current level of CO2 in grams/liter
    (link to Braukaiser)
  • Transform grams/liter into Volumes
    (link to Science direct)
  • Calculate the amount of sugar to add
    (link to Braukaiser)

Breakdown of equations:

  • Cbeer is the amount of CO2 in the beer in grams/liter
  • Phead is the head pressure in the beer in Bar
  • Tbeer is the temperature of the beer in Celsius
  • Cbeer_volumes is the amount of CO2 in the beer in Volumes

  • msugar is the amount of table sugar in grams
  • Cdesired_co2 is the desired level of CO2 in grams/liter
  • Cbeer is the current amount of CO2 in the beer in grams/liter
  • Vbeer is the beer volume in liters
